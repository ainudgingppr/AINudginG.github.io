# About This Project

## AI Nudging for Sustainable Tourism: Mechanisms, Boundaries, and Ethical Implications

### 🌍 Project Background
Artificial Intelligence (AI) is reshaping consumer behavior, offering new opportunities to address sustainability challenges in the tourism and hospitality industry. This research project investigates the potential of AI nudging to promote sustainable customer behavior, exploring its efficacy compared to traditional nudging methods. The study aims to bridge critical gaps in understanding how AI can be ethically and effectively deployed to support sustainability goals.

---

### 🔍 Research Focus & Hypotheses
This research addresses key gaps in the field, where existing studies have largely focused on traditional nudges rather than the mechanisms and boundary conditions of AI nudging. The project tests five core hypotheses:
1.  AI nudging triggers more sustainable behavior than traditional nudging.
2.  Perceived relevance mediates the positive effect of AI nudging.
3.  Perceived autonomy moderates the influence of AI nudging, making it more effective under high autonomy and less effective under low autonomy.
4.  AI disclosure moderates the influence of AI nudging, weakening its impact when disclosed and strengthening it when undisclosed.
5.  Perceived goal alignment further moderates the effect, enhancing AI nudging when goals align with the user, and weakening it when aligned with the nudger (e.g., company profit).

---

### 📊 Multi-Study Research Design
To test these hypotheses, the project adopts a multi-study, mixed-methods design:
- **Study 1**: Natural experiment using Difference-in-Differences (DiD) analysis on secondary data to evaluate the real-world impact of AI nudging interventions.
- **Study 2**: Controlled comparison of AI vs. traditional nudging in an e-commerce context, examining perceived relevance as a mediating mechanism.
- **Study 3**: Manipulation of perceived autonomy to assess how freedom of choice shapes behavioral responses to AI nudging.
- **Study 4**: Examination of AI disclosure’s moderating role on autonomy and sustainability outcomes.
- **Study 5**: Field experiment in the tourism and hospitality sector, manipulating perceived goal alignment to test AI nudging in real-world service environments.

---

### 🎯 Expected Contributions
Collectively, this research provides new theoretical and empirical insights into when, how, and for whom AI nudging effectively promotes sustainable behavior. It aims to:
- Enrich academic understanding of AI-mediated behavioral interventions.
- Offer practical guidance for policymakers and industry practitioners on designing responsible, autonomy-supportive AI systems.
- Advance sustainable consumption by clarifying the conditions under which AI nudging can ethically and effectively influence consumer decision-making.
